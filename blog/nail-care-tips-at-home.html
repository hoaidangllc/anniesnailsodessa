Nail Care Tips at Home

Healthy nails do not only depend on salon visits. Good habits at home can help keep your nails stronger, cleaner, and looking better between appointments. A few simple steps can make a big difference in your overall nail health.

At Annie's Nails & Spa, we always encourage clients to take care of their hands and nails between visits for longer lasting results.

Keep Nails Clean and Dry

Keeping your nails clean and dry helps prevent dirt buildup and reduces the risk of weak or damaged nails. After washing your hands, make sure to dry around the nails and cuticles well.

Use Cuticle Oil and Hand Lotion

Moisture is one of the best things for healthy nails. Dry cuticles can lead to peeling and rough skin, so applying cuticle oil and hand lotion regularly helps keep nails looking smooth and healthy.

Avoid Using Nails as Tools

Using your nails to open cans, scrape labels, or pick at surfaces can weaken them over time. Treating your nails gently helps them stay stronger and last longer.

Wear Gloves for Cleaning

Household cleaners and long exposure to water can dry out your hands and nails. Wearing gloves while cleaning or washing dishes helps protect your nails and skin.

Visit the Salon Regularly

Home care is important, but regular professional nail care also helps maintain healthy nails. Manicures, pedicures, gel polish, and acrylic maintenance are easier when your nails are cared for consistently.
